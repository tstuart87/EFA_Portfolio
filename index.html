I grew up in West Lafayette, IN and later attended Franklin College where I earned a B.A. in History.

After college I began working in agricultural research where I carved out a niche as the computer guy, mostly because I was really bad at driving tractors. I quickly fell in love with creating tools using nothing but Microsoft Excel that would help others do their jobs more effectively. I was building clever tools that were very useful - they helped our team become more accurate and efficient, but there was only so far I could go in Excel.

A few years into my career I took an online bootcamp focusing on data analysis using Python and SQL - this is when I really dove into the coding world. I could analyze data more effectively, but what really excited me was automating repetitive tasks that myself and other team members were doing in our never ending quest for data collection and data entry.

A few years after the online bootcamp, I decided I wanted to go back to school to earn another bachelor's degree - this time in computer science.

About half-way through my computer science program, I realized that I wanted to be a developer. I wanted to combine this new coding knowledge with the old Microsoft Excel tools I had built. I wanted to build applications to help other people do their jobs more successfully.

The .NET Immersion bootcamp at the Eleven Fifty Academy was the obvious choice. There I was given the opportunity to learn the tools needed to be a successful developer, and get real world experience.
